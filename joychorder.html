    JoyChorder is an interface for driving text inputs with a standard gamepad's thumbsticks.

    Instead of awkwardly positioning a cursor over a virtual keyboard to select individual characters, JoyChorder allows users to quickly & comfortably select from over 200 character sequences by combining motions of the thumbsticks & using the triggers as quasimodal modifiers.

    By relying on about 80 distinct motions (64 pairs of directions + 8 individual directions per stick) instead of grid-navigating or menu-diving, with practice the simple-to-execute inputs should become strongly associated with the character sequences they produce through muscle memory.

    With each input having its own kinesthetic shape, each character selection requiring only one simultaneous action (rather than indicating a group of letters & then confirming a selection from that subset), room on the controller left over for handy editing commands, and the ability to select individual characters as well as strings of characters including whole words, JoyChorder takes full advantage of the affordances of the gamepad to provide a powerful & satisfying typing experience.
